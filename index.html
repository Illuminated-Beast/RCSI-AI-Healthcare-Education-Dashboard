What the Evidence Scores Mean

Every topic in this dashboard is scored on three dimensions, verified directly from Stage6_Evidence_Scoring.xlsx. These are not estimates — they are the exact scoring rules the pipeline applied.

Dimension Weight What it measures Thresholds
D1 — Outcome Reporting 50% % of papers in the topic reporting a measurable outcome (Stage 4 regex flag) HIGH ≥80% · MEDIUM 60–79% · LOW <60%
D2 — Design Quality 30% % using rigorous designs — RCT, systematic review, benchmarking, cohort (Stage 5) HIGH ≥20% · MEDIUM 10–19% · LOW <10%
D3 — Vocabulary Maturity 20% Ratio of evaluative to exploratory keywords (15 keywords/topic — supplementary signal) HIGH ≥0.5 · MEDIUM 0.2–0.49 or no signal · LOW <0.2

Composite = D1×0.5 + D2×0.3 + D3×0.2  →  HIGH ≥2.5 · MEDIUM 1.7–2.49 · LOW <1.7

Known limitation, stated explicitly in the source rubric: AI Benchmarking & Exam Performance scores HIGH on D1 (94.0%) but LOW on D2 (7.8%) — these papers report quantitative accuracy results (caught by D1) but rarely use formal design terms like "benchmark" (D2). Its MEDIUM composite (2.4) reflects this measurement tension, not a true evidence-quality deficit. Also note: D3 is a real, measured signal for only 2 of the 6 topics — the other 4 have zero evaluative/exploratory keyword hits and receive the "no signal → MEDIUM" default, not an actual vocabulary measurement.

How This Dashboard Works

This page explains the research pipeline behind the dashboard and how the dashboard itself displays that output. It is linked from the dashboard rather than shown inline, so the dashboard stays focused on findings rather than methodology.

What the dashboard shows

All charts and tables are aggregate statistics computed from a locked, six-topic structure derived from BERTopic clustering of the full corpus. The evidence scores (D1/D2/D3, composite, tier) were verified once against that locked structure and are not recomputed on the fly — they are fixed reference values so that repeated viewings of the dashboard always show the same, already-verified figures. No paper-level data (titles, authors, links) is included in or accessible from this dashboard.

Research Pipeline — Stages 0 to 7 (complete)
STAGE 0

Data Preparation

Filtered 1,529 screening rows and 2,193 combined results to 3 target categories. Deduplication produced 507 screening and 506 combined records. Abstract recovery flagged 17+79 missing abstracts.

STAGE 1

Corpus Construction

Python/Pandas pipeline: normalised 9 raw classification variants → 7 canonical. Extracted years from mixed date formats. Built Text_for_NLP field (Title + Annotation + Abstract). Added Outcome_Reported regex flag.

STAGE 2

BERTopic NLP Modelling

BERTopic with TF-IDF-based embeddings, KMeans backend (not HDBSCAN — Windows C++ compatibility). 507 documents. A 6-topic run with domain stopwords removed produced the coherent, final, locked cluster structure used throughout this dashboard.

STAGE 3

Statistical Analysis

Chi-square tests with Cramér's V effect sizes. Post-hoc adjusted residuals. Fisher's exact test. Linear regression. Output: stats_summary.xlsx.

STAGE 4

Exploratory Data Analysis

Publication trend visualisation, keyword vocabulary shift analysis, and descriptive profiling across the locked topic structure.

STAGE 5

Gap Identification

Study-design classification and gap-matrix construction across topics and disciplines. RCT count presented as a range (strict PICO vs broad inclusion criteria) rather than a single figure.

STAGE 6

Evidence Scoring

D1 (outcome reporting), D2 (rigorous design), D3 (vocabulary maturity) computed per topic; composite = D1×0.5 + D2×0.3 + D3×0.2. Verified once, then locked as the reference values this dashboard displays.

STAGE 7

Synthesis

Cross-cutting synthesis of findings, headline gaps, and recommendations, feeding the dissertation Discussion section.
