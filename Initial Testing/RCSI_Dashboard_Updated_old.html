Primary finding — D1/D2 inversion (Topic 1: AI Benchmarking)

D1 = 94.0% HIGH (highest in corpus) paired with D2 = 7.8% LOW. Papers measure AI accuracy on MCQ and licensing exams extensively but fewer than 1 in 12 use an experimental or systematic design. Replicated across three BERTopic re-fits — this is a property of the literature, not a modelling artefact. Composite 2.4 MEDIUM.

Reverse inversion — Topic 2: AI in Medical Imaging

D2 = 29.9% HIGH (highest in corpus — uses systematic reviews and surgical cohort studies) paired with D1 = 50.5% LOW. Good methodology, but half the papers do not report a measurable outcome. Different failure mode from Topic 1. Composite 1.8 MEDIUM.

Third inversion — Topic 5: Perceptions, Readiness & Knowledge-Attitude

D1 = 82.1% HIGH (second highest in corpus) paired with D2 = 3.6% LOW (lowest in entire corpus). Knowledge-attitude surveys reliably report attitudinal outcomes but almost universally avoid formal experimental designs. D3 also LOW — composite 2.0 MEDIUM despite high D1.

↕ Inv D1 HIGH / D2 LOW — measures outcomes without rigorous designs ↕ Rev D1 LOW / D2 HIGH — rigorous designs without outcome reporting
